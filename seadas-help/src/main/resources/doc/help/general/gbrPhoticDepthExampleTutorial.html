SeaDAS-7 in Action: Photic Depth Composite Imagery on the Great Barrier Reef

Using SeaDAS 7 to create a custom composite mapped image of satellite-derived photic depth on the Great Barrier Reef.

Objective and Results

The above image was created using SeaDAS 7. This image is essentially a recreation of an image and finding contained with (citation) which observes that this figure "shows strong negative" Secchi depth "anomalies from 12 to 17 September 2008 following intrusions of clear oceanic waters onto the shelf" of the Great Barrier Reef, Australia. The primary data used are MODIS AQUA and SeaWIFS level1 files, Feb 12-17 2008. To obtain a nominal mean, MODIS AQUA level1 files are used from Feb 12-17 for the years 2002-2013. The specifics of this case study is to create a mapped image based on satellite-derived water clarity data for the central-southern Great Barrier Reef. The region of study is limited to areas within the 200m isobath. The primary data being used are MODIS AQUA level1 files, Feb 12-17 2008. In addition, a nominal mean is determined using MODIS AQUA level1 files from Feb 12-17 for the years 2002-2013.

  • Create (0m to -200m) bathymetry mask
  • Create region of interest boundaries shapefile (vector container)
  • Create Secchi depth anomalies product (limited by bathymetry mask and region of interest)
  • Add custom color palette to Secchi depth anomalies product
  • Rotate Secchi depth anomalies product
  • Create rotated region of interest boundaries shapefile (vector container)
  • Add coastline
  • Add -200m and -1000m isobath lines
  • Add gridlines
  • Create color legend
  • Create topography product
  • Create bathymetry product
  • Create bathymetry and topography products (further limited by masks)
  • Add color palettes to bathymetry, topography
  • Layer bathymetry and topography onto Secchi depth anomalies product
  • Adjust bathymetry and topography layer transparency

Merge the products into a single product

In order to more easily work with the data, merge these SeaDAS products into a single SeaDAS product. This is done with the collocation tool. [Tools -> Collocation] The collocation tool only merges two products at a time, so it will be necessary to collocate twice in order to get all three products merged. First we merge L3map_1km_elev_20022013 and L3map_1km_Zp_10_lee_20022013 into a single product L3map_1km_composite_tmp. Note that the band names for photic depth are identical in the products L3map_1km_Zp_10_lee_20022013 and L3map_1km_Zp_10_lee_2008, so we modify the resultant band names by adding a suffix in order to retain desired source data information. Also note that the choice of which product is master can make a difference, Essentially the slave product dimensions will be trimmed down to fit the master products dimensions.

Next we merge the L3map_1km_Zp_10_lee_2008 product with L3map_1km_composite_tmp to produce L3map_1km_composite, again modifying the product names as desired.
